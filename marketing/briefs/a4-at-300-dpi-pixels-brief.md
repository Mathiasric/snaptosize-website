### Main Content Sections

1. **Why 300 DPI Is the Print Standard**
   - 300 DPI = 300 dots per inch — the benchmark for offset and digital print quality
   - At 300 DPI, individual pixels are invisible to the naked eye at normal viewing distance
   - Lower DPI (72–150) is fine for screens, but prints appear blurry or pixelated
   - Most professional print shops require 300 DPI minimum for close-up viewing (books, art prints, planners)
   - Etsy customers downloading printables expect 300 DPI files they can print at home or via a print service

2. **A4 at 300 DPI — The Exact Dimensions**
   - Portrait: **2480 × 3508 pixels** (width × height)
   - Landscape: **3508 × 2480 pixels**
   - Physical size: 8.27 × 11.69 inches / 210 × 297 mm
   - Formula: inches × 300 = pixels (e.g. 8.27 × 300 = 2481 ≈ 2480 rounded to standard)
   - Use these exact pixel values when setting up your canvas in Photoshop, Illustrator, Canva, or Affinity

3. **A3, 24×36 and More — Full Reference Table**
   - A3 at 300 DPI: 3508 × 4961 pixels — double the area of A4, common for large posters
   - 24×36 at 300 DPI: 7200 × 10800 pixels — standard US poster size, very large file
   - A2 at 300 DPI: 4961 × 7016 pixels — used for exhibition prints and signage
   - A1 at 300 DPI: 7016 × 9933 pixels — requires very high source resolution
   - 18×24 at 300 DPI: 5400 × 7200 pixels — popular US wall art and poster size
   - See full table below for all sizes

4. **How to Set Up a 300 DPI Canvas in Your Design Tool**
   - **Photoshop:** New Document → set width/height in pixels → Resolution: 300 pixels/inch
   - **Illustrator:** New Document → set artboard in mm (210×297) → export at 300 DPI
   - **Canva:** Use "Custom size" → enter pixels directly (2480 × 3508) → download as high-res PDF or JPEG
   - **Affinity Designer/Photo:** New → Document Setup → DPI: 300, set dimensions in mm or pixels
   - Always work in the pixel dimensions from the start — resizing up after the fact loses quality

5. **Exporting Print-Ready Files for Etsy**
   - Etsy accepts JPEG, PNG, and PDF formats for digital downloads
   - JPEG at 300 DPI is the standard for wall art printables — compact and universally printable
   - Keep file size under 20 MB (Etsy's upload limit) — a 2480×3508 JPEG auto-optimizes well within this
   - Offer multiple sizes in one listing: A4, A3, A5 at minimum for international buyers

### FAQ (5-7 questions)

**Q1: What is A4 at 300 DPI in pixels?**
A: A4 at 300 DPI is 2480 × 3508 pixels in portrait orientation (width × height). In landscape it's 3508 × 2480 pixels. The physical A4 size is 210 × 297 mm (8.27 × 11.69 inches).

**Q2: What is A3 at 300 DPI in pixels?**
A: A3 at 300 DPI is 3508 × 4961 pixels (portrait). A3 is exactly double the area of A4 — two A4 sheets side by side. It's commonly used for posters, large art prints, and architectural drawings.

**Q3: What are 24×36 pixels at 300 DPI?**
A: A 24×36 inch print at 300 DPI requires 7200 × 10800 pixels. This is a very large file (approx. 78 megapixels) used for large-format posters and gallery-sized wall art. For Etsy, this size is rare — most buyers order A3 or 16×20 instead.

**Q4: Do I need exactly 300 DPI for Etsy printables?**
A: Yes — 300 DPI is the minimum recommended resolution for print-quality files. Etsy customers print your files at home or via services like Walgreens, Staples, or professional print labs. At 300 DPI, prints look sharp and professional at the listed size. Files at 72 or 96 DPI will print blurry.

**Q5: How do I convert inches to pixels at 300 DPI?**
A: Multiply each dimension in inches by 300. Example: A4 = 8.27" × 11.69" → 8.27 × 300 = 2481 ≈ 2480 px, 11.69 × 300 = 3507 ≈ 3508 px. The slight rounding is standard — most design tools use 2480 × 3508 for A4.

**Q6: Can I resize an A4 file to A3 without losing quality?**
A: Only if your source file is large enough. If your A4 file is already at 2480 × 3508 px (300 DPI), resizing up to A3 (3508 × 4961 px) will reduce the effective DPI to about 212 — which may look slightly soft. **Q7: What's the file size of an A4 at 300 DPI JPEG?**
