## FAQ Items (6)

1. **Q: What is the best size for Etsy listing photos in 2026?**
   A: Etsy recommends at least 2000x2000 pixels. For the sharpest zoom experience, upload at 3000x3000px. Keep file size under 1MB for fast loading. Square format works best since Etsy crops thumbnails to square on mobile search results.

2. **Q: Are listing photos the same as my digital download files?**
   A: No. Listing photos are the preview images buyers see when browsing your shop (72 DPI, 2000-3000px, optimized for web). Digital download files are the high-resolution print files customers receive after purchase (300 DPI, much larger pixel dimensions like 2400x3000px for an 8x10). Never upload your print files as listing photos.

3. **Q: Why does my listing photo look blurry on Etsy?**
   A: Etsy compresses uploaded images for fast web display. If your source image is at least 2000px on the shortest side, the zoom feature will work and the image will look sharp. Images under 2000px cannot zoom and appear soft. Also check that you are exporting in sRGB color profile.

4. **Q: What aspect ratio should I use for Etsy listing photos?**
   A: Square (1:1) is safest because Etsy crops thumbnails to square on mobile and 4:3 on desktop. If you prefer rectangular, use 4:3 landscape and keep the main subject centered to survive cropping. Avoid tall portrait-oriented listing photos — they get heavily cropped in search.

5. **Q: How many listing photos should I use on Etsy?**
   A: Use all 10 slots. Listings with more photos convert better. Use slot 1 for a hero mockup, slots 2-5 for lifestyle shots in different rooms, slots 6-8 for size variants, and slots 9-10 for a "what's included" graphic and size chart.

6. **Q: Do I need different listing photos for each print size I sell?**
   A: For digital downloads offering multiple sizes (like 5x7 through 24x36), you don't need separate listing photos for each size. Instead, use one listing photo as a "what's included" graphic showing all available sizes. Your mockup photos should show the art in its most popular size (usually 8x10 or 16x20) in a realistic room setting.
