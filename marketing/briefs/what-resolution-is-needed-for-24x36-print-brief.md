### Intro (100–150 words)
A 24×36 print is one of the most popular large-format poster sizes — used for art prints, photography, movie-style posters, and decorative wall art. But printing at this size requires a file that's actually large enough. Upload something too small and you'll get a blurry, pixelated result that no print shop or Etsy customer will accept.

The short answer: you need **7200 × 10800 pixels** at **300 DPI** for a professional-quality 24×36 print. That's the industry standard for artwork meant to be viewed up close. If your file falls short of that, you have options — but you need to understand the trade-offs first. This guide gives you the exact numbers, a full reference table, and a tool to resize or repackage your file correctly.

### Main Content Sections

#### 1. The Exact Resolution for a 24×36 Print
- 300 DPI (dots per inch) is the professional print standard — required by most print shops and Etsy print-on-demand services
- At 300 DPI: **7200 × 10800 pixels** — this is the gold standard
- At 150 DPI: **3600 × 5400 pixels** — acceptable for large-format prints viewed from 3+ feet away (posters on a wall)
- At 72 DPI: **1728 × 2592 pixels** — screen resolution only, NOT suitable for printing at 24×36
- DPI = pixels ÷ inches: 24 × 300 = 7200px wide, 36 × 300 = 10800px tall
- Always check both pixel dimensions — width AND height must meet the requirement

#### 2. Why 300 DPI Matters at This Size
- At 24×36, viewers often stand close — fine art buyers, gallery prints, photography — so pixelation is visible
- 150 DPI is a common threshold for large-format posters (think: trade show banners, retail signage) viewed from a distance
- Etsy digital downloads must be print-ready — if buyers download your file and print it blurry, you'll get complaints and refund requests
- Print-on-demand services (Printify, Printful, Gelato) typically require 300 DPI at final print size
- Going below 150 DPI at 24×36 is not recommended under any circumstances

#### 3. How to Check Your File's Resolution
- In Photoshop: Image → Image Size — check pixel dimensions AND resolution (PPI)
- In Preview (Mac): Tools → Show Inspector → Image DPI
- Quick math: divide your pixel width by 24 (inches) — if the result is ≥ 300, you're good
- Example: a 6000 × 9000 px file = 6000 ÷ 24 = 250 DPI at 24×36 — slightly under, but acceptable for many large-format uses
- A 7200 × 10800 px file = exactly 300 DPI — fully print-ready
- File size indicator: a proper 300 DPI 24×36 JPEG at auto-optimized quality is typically 8–25 MB

#### 4. What If Your File Isn't Large Enough?
- Upscaling a low-res file in Photoshop (or AI upscaling tools) can recover some detail, but won't fix a fundamentally small image
- The safest approach: create or export your artwork at the correct pixel dimensions from the start
- For Etsy sellers: SnapToSize auto-resizes your artwork to exact pixel dimensions at 300 DPI — no Photoshop required
- If you have a 20×30 or 18×24 file, you're close — check the resolution table below for exact numbers
- Never just "stretch" a file in a design app — that resamples incorrectly and produces blurry results

#### 5. 24×36 vs. Related Print Sizes
- 24×36 is a 2:3 ratio (same ratio as 4×6, 8×12, 16×24, 20×30)
- If you design for 24×36, the same artwork crops/resizes cleanly to 20×30, 16×24, 12×18, 8×12, 4×6
- This makes 24×36 a smart "master size" for Etsy sellers — design once, resize to the full 2:3 lineup
- For a 3:4 ratio (18×24), the aspect ratio is different — you'd need to adjust composition

### FAQ (6 questions)

**Q: What resolution do I need for a 24×36 print?**
A: You need 7200 × 10800 pixels at 300 DPI for a professional-quality 24×36 print. This is the standard required by most print shops, Etsy print-on-demand services, and fine art printers. For large-format posters viewed from a distance, 3600 × 5400 pixels at 150 DPI is the minimum acceptable resolution.

**Q: What is 24x36 in pixels at 300 DPI?**
A: 24×36 inches at 300 DPI equals 7200 × 10800 pixels. This is calculated by multiplying each dimension by the DPI: 24 × 300 = 7200 pixels wide, and 36 × 300 = 10800 pixels tall.

**Q: Is 150 DPI good enough for a 24×36 poster?**
A: 150 DPI (3600 × 5400 pixels) is acceptable for large-format posters that will be viewed from 3 feet or more — like a wall poster, trade show banner, or retail display. For fine art prints, photography prints, or any print that buyers will view up close, 300 DPI is strongly recommended.

**Q: Can I print a 24×36 poster from a phone photo?**
A: Most smartphone photos are 12–50 megapixels, but the pixel dimensions depend on the specific phone. A 12MP photo is roughly 4000 × 3000 pixels — in landscape orientation, that's only 111 DPI at 24×36, which is too low. A 48MP or 50MP phone photo may be borderline. Always check your pixel dimensions before ordering a 24×36 print.

**Q: What aspect ratio is 24×36?**
A: 24×36 is a 2:3 aspect ratio (the same ratio as 4×6, 8×12, 16×24, and 20×30). This is one of the most common print ratios and matches the native ratio of full-frame DSLR cameras. If your artwork is designed for 24×36, it can be resized to any other 2:3 size without cropping.
