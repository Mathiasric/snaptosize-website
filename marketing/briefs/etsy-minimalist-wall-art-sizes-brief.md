## FAQ topics (6 questions)

1. **What is the best-selling minimalist wall art size on Etsy?**
   Answer: 8x10 (2400x3000px at 300 DPI) is the #1 seller overall, but minimalist art over-indexes on large statement sizes. 16x20 and 24x36 perform exceptionally well because buyers use minimalist designs as focal points in curated living spaces. Offering at least 5-8 sizes captures the full market.

2. **How many sizes should I include in a minimalist art listing?**
   Answer: Top minimalist art sellers offer 8-15 sizes per listing. At minimum, include 5x7, 8x10, 11x14, 16x20, 24x36, A4, and A3. More sizes increase perceived value, reduce "do you have this in..." messages, and improve Etsy search visibility.

3. **Should minimalist prints be portrait or landscape?**
   Answer: Most minimalist wall art on Etsy is portrait (vertical) orientation, but offering both from the same design can effectively double your catalog. Landscape versions work well for above-sofa placement. SnapToSize generates both portrait and landscape from the same upload.

4. **What sizes work best for minimalist gallery wall sets?**
   Answer: The most popular gallery wall sizes for minimalist sets are 8x10, 11x14, and 16x20 — all prints in a set should match. Offering the set in 3 size options (small/medium/large) in a single listing maximizes buyer choice. Consistent sizing is even more critical for minimalist sets because the clean aesthetic demands precision.

5. **Do minimalist art buyers need international (A-series) sizes?**
   Answer: Yes, especially for Scandinavian and Japandi styles that have a strong following in Northern Europe, UK, and Australia. A4 and A3 are standard frame sizes in these markets. Skipping A-series means missing 20-30% of potential buyers. SnapToSize's ISO pack generates A5 through A1 automatically.

6. **What resolution do I need for large minimalist prints like 24x36?**
   Answer: 300 DPI is the professional standard. At 24x36, that's 7200x10800 pixels. Minimalist art with large areas of solid color or negative space reveals pixelation more easily than detailed art, so 300 DPI is non-negotiable. SnapToSize outputs every file at 300 DPI.
