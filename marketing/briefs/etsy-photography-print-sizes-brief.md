## FAQ Items (6)

1. **Q: What is the best print size for photography on Etsy?**
   A: For photographers, the 2:3 ratio family (4x6 through 20x30) is ideal because it matches the native 3:2 sensor ratio of most DSLR and mirrorless cameras — zero cropping needed. However, you should also include 4:5 sizes (especially 8x10 and 16x20) since those are the most common frame sizes buyers already own. Offering both ratio families maximizes your addressable market.

2. **Q: Do I need to crop my photos for every print size?**
   A: Not for sizes in your camera's native ratio. If you shoot on a full-frame or APS-C camera (3:2 ratio), sizes like 4x6, 8x12, 12x18, 16x24, and 20x30 need no cropping at all. Sizes in other ratios (like 8x10 at 4:5) will require some cropping — about 16% of the image width for 3:2-to-4:5 conversion.

3. **Q: What resolution do I need for large photography prints?**
   A: For print-quality output at 300 DPI, a 20x30 inch print requires 6000x9000 pixels. Most modern cameras (24MP+) can handle this natively. For a 16x24 print, you need 4800x7200 pixels. Always export from Lightroom or Camera RAW at maximum resolution before resizing for each print size.

4. **Q: Should I include ISO/A-series sizes for my photography prints?**
   A: Yes, especially if you sell to international buyers. A4 and A3 are the most popular paper sizes outside the US. The A-series uses a 1:1.414 ratio, which is between 3:2 and 4:3, so minimal cropping is needed from either camera format. Including ISO sizes opens up the European, Australian, and Asian markets.

5. **Q: What is the difference between photography print sizes and art print sizes?**
   A: The key difference is the starting ratio. Cameras output in 3:2 (DSLR/mirrorless) or 4:3 (Micro Four Thirds/phones), while digital artists typically design at whatever ratio they choose — often 4:5 for the popular 8x10 size. This means photographers face a cropping challenge that artists do not. Photography sellers should lead with the 2:3 ratio pack and add 4:5 sizes as a complement, while art sellers often lead with 4:5.

6. **Q: How do I handle panoramic and ultra-wide photography for Etsy?**
   A: Panoramic shots (16:9 or wider) don't fit standard print sizes well. Your best options are: offer them as-is with specific panoramic dimensions (e.g., 12x36, 10x30), crop to 2:3 for standard sizes (losing significant top/bottom), or include them as bonus files alongside your standard ratio packs. Many sellers offer panoramics as separate listings rather than including them in multi-size bundles.
