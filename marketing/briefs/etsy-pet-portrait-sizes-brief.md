## FAQ topics (6 questions)

1. **What is the best-selling pet portrait size on Etsy?**
   Answer: 8x10 is the #1 seller for pet portraits, followed closely by 11x14. However, memorial pet portraits and royal/renaissance styles over-index on larger sizes like 16x20 and 18x24, where buyers want the portrait displayed prominently. Offering at minimum 5x7, 8x10, 11x14, and 16x20 covers 80%+ of buyer demand.

2. **How many sizes should I offer for a pet portrait listing?**
   Answer: Top pet portrait sellers offer 8-12 sizes per listing. At minimum include 5x7, 8x10, 11x14, 16x20, and A4. More sizes increase perceived value and reduce "do you have this in X size?" messages. SnapToSize generates all standard sizes from one upload.

3. **Should pet portraits be vertical or horizontal?**
   Answer: Single pet portraits (head and shoulders) are almost always vertical/portrait orientation — this matches how we naturally frame a face. Full-body portraits of long dogs (dachshunds, greyhounds) may work better horizontal. Multi-pet portraits with 2-3 pets side by side work well in landscape or square formats. Offer both orientations as separate listings to maximize catalog coverage.

4. **What size is best for a pet memorial portrait?**
   Answer: Memorial pet portraits are purchased as meaningful tributes, so buyers lean toward larger sizes: 11x14, 16x20, and 18x24 are the most popular for memorials. These sizes are large enough to be a room focal point without being overwhelming. Always include at least one large format option (16x20+) in memorial portrait listings.

5. **Do I need square sizes for pet portraits?**
   Answer: Yes, if you sell pop art, cartoon, or Instagram-style pet portraits. Square formats (10x10, 12x12, 16x16) are popular for these sub-styles because they match social media aesthetics and modern gallery wall trends. For traditional and watercolor portraits, square is less common — 4:5 and 3:4 ratios dominate.

6. **What resolution do I need for large pet portrait prints?**
   Answer: 300 DPI is the industry standard for print-quality output. For a 16x20 print, that means your source file needs to be at least 4800x6000 pixels. For 24x36, you need 7200x10800 pixels. Start with the highest resolution source possible — SnapToSize maintains 300 DPI across all output sizes, but cannot upscale a low-resolution original.
