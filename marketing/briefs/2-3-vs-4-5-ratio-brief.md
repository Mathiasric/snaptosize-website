## FAQ topics (5 questions):

1. **What ratio is an 8x10 print?** — 8x10 is a 4:5 ratio. Many sellers mistakenly think 8x10 is the same ratio as 4x6 (which is 2:3). They are different ratios and require separate source files to avoid cropping.

2. **Can I crop a 2:3 design to fit a 4:5 frame?** — Technically yes, but you lose 16.7% of the image width. For wall art with intentional composition (borders, centered text, balanced whitespace), cropping destroys the design. Professional sellers create dedicated files for each ratio.

3. **Which ratio sells better on Etsy — 2:3 or 4:5?** — Both are essential. 2:3 drives large poster sales (24x36, 16x24), while 4:5 drives traditional frame sales (8x10, 16x20). Top Etsy printable listings include both ratios plus 3:4 and ISO sizes. Omitting either costs you sales.

4. **Do I also need 3:4 and ISO ratios?** — Yes, for maximum coverage. 3:4 covers sizes like 12x16 and 18x24. ISO (A-series) covers international buyers who print on A4, A3, and A2 paper. Most top sellers include 5 ratio packs total. SnapToSize generates all 5 from a single upload.

5. **How many print sizes should I include per Etsy listing?** — Professional sellers include 25-30 sizes across 5 ratios. This sounds overwhelming to create manually, but tools like SnapToSize generate all sizes from one image in under 30 seconds. More sizes = fewer customer questions = higher conversion rates.
