## FAQ Topics (7 questions)

1. **What size should I make my digital planner for Etsy?** — Offer at least US Letter (8.5x11), A4, and A5 to cover US and international buyers. These three sizes capture the widest audience with minimal extra work.

2. **What is the difference between A4 and US Letter for planners?** — A4 (210x297mm / 8.27x11.69") is slightly narrower and taller than US Letter (8.5x11"). A4 is the international standard; Letter is the US standard. Offering both ensures worldwide buyers can print correctly.

3. **What DPI should I use for printable planners on Etsy?** — 300 DPI for any planner page that will be physically printed. Digital-only planners (GoodNotes/Notability) can use 150 DPI since they display on screens, but 300 DPI works for both use cases.

4. **What are the pixel dimensions for A5 planner pages at 300 DPI?** — A5 at 300 DPI is 1748x2480 pixels (148x210mm). This is the most popular compact planner size and fits standard A5 binders and planners.

5. **Can I sell both digital planners and wall art on the same Etsy shop?** — Yes, and many successful shops do. Planner designs (typography, quotes, minimalist patterns) translate directly to wall art. Offering both product types increases your shop's keyword visibility and average order value.

6. **What wall art sizes should I add if I already sell planners?** — Start with the sizes you already know: A4, A5, and 8.5x11 (US Letter). Then expand to popular frame sizes like 8x10, 5x7, 11x14, 16x20, and 24x36. SnapToSize generates 30+ sizes from a single upload.

7. **How do I resize one design into multiple planner and print sizes?** — For planner pages, create at your largest size (US Letter or A4) and scale down. For wall art, upload your artwork once to SnapToSize and get up to 70 print-ready files across all standard frame sizes at 300 DPI.
