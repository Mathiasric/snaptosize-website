# SnapToSize — Competitor Landscape

Where SnapToSize sits vs alternatives, what we cover, and where the gaps/opportunities are.
For positioning, vs-pages, and roadmap decisions. Last updated: 2026-06-19 (post Perfect Fit
launch). Companion: `marketing/MODES_REFERENCE.md`.

---

## The headline

**There is no direct SaaS competitor** for our core job: *one upload → every Etsy print size,
distortion-free*. Competitors cluster into categories, none of which do what we do:

| Category | Examples | What they do | Gap vs us |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generic online resizers | Soona, IMResizer, Instasize, PixelBatch | Resize one image to a chosen spec/format (often free, no signup) | Commodity **single** resize — no pack, no distortion-free crop |
| Design tools | Canva, Photoshop | Manual canvas; you do the work | Slow, manual, no Etsy-specific automation |
| Photoshop plugins | Bulk Mockup | Resize to all ratios **+** mockups | Requires Photoshop; not a SaaS |
| Mockup tools | Placeit, Creative Market, Envato, Canva | Room-scene art-in-frame listing images | Different *product* (mockups, not sizing) |

**On *sizing*, SnapToSize is the most complete option** — and the only one with
distortion-free focal-crop to every Etsy size.

## Our moat (in order of defensibility)

1. **Perfect Fit (distortion-free focal crop to every Etsy size)** — *nobody else does this.*
   The ownable wedge. No competitor owns the "crop art to print ratio without distortion" intent.
2. **The pack model** (one upload → every size as a bundle) — few do it; the closest (Bulk
   Mockup) is a Photoshop plugin, not SaaS.
3. **Etsy-specific opinionation** — we know the sizes/ratios/limits; generic tools are neutral
   racks the user has to drive.

**Commodity (not a differentiator):** single-image resize. Everyone has it. This is why
single-export-crop is a low-ceiling add, not a moat — see roadmap.

## Gaps / opportunities (what rivals offer that we don't)

These are **adjacent jobs**, not sizing — separate product bets, in priority order:

1. **Mockups** (room-scene art-in-frame) — the #1 recurring rival offering (Bulk Mockup,
   Canva, Placeit, Creative Market). Etsy sellers need listing images. Biggest expansion bet.
   Memory: `project_mockup_opportunity`.
2. **Batch / bulk** (many artworks at once) — PixelBatch does it; we do one artwork at a time.
   Real power-seller need. Big-bet.
3. **Personalization** — within sizing, **My Packs is already our personalization layer**
   (saved size sets). Rivals' "personalization" is mostly niche mockup templates, not sizing —
   we're not behind here.

## Per-mode SEO / search-intent angles (for vs-pages + landing pages)

- **Perfect Fit (highest opportunity — ownable, low competition):** "crop art to print size
  without distortion," "reframe art to different aspect ratio," "distortion-free resize for print."
- **Size Packs (core, high-intent):** "Etsy print size pack," "resize art to all Etsy sizes."
- **Quick Export:** "resize image to [size] for print."
- **My Packs:** "save custom print size set."

## Positioning guardrails

- Lead acquisition with the **simple core promise** ("one upload, all Etsy sizes"). Don't
  fragment the homepage into "here are our 4 modes" — that re-creates decision paralysis.
- Go deep on modes via **dedicated sub-pages** (Perfect Fit first — it capitalizes on the
  launch and owns an uncontested intent).
- Frame everything by **what we're good for**, never by a rival's weakness or our own
  trade-offs (see `MODES_REFERENCE.md` golden rule).
